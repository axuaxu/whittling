The Essential Guide for Wood Whittling Beginners


Welcome to Whittling.Us, the site specializes to the ancient craft of wood carving and whittling. Wood whittling is one of the oldest crafts in the world, it had been practiced across continents from Europe, to Asia, to Africa for thousands of years.

Nowadays, wood whittling is becoming an incredibly rewarding and relexing hobby. With very basic tools, you can transform your ideas into beautiful work of art, create something amazingly beautiful, or something that is functional.


To get started, this article will be focusing on the basic techniques and tools for whittling beginners.


What is Whittling?

Whittling is the art and style of woodcarving performed using a carving knife. Whittling normally makes a sculpture oppose to adding detail to a piece, a trademark of whittling is that the knife strokes are clearly visible on the final piece giving it a rugged natural feel.

Choosing the Right Tools

There are many techniques of wood carving, whittling, chip carving, relief carving etc. Whittling is the most easy to start, chip carving is relatively easy while relief carving is more advanced. Wood carving are for more complicated wood works, wood carving uses of chisels, gouges, knives and a mallet.


Whittling is the most basic and most popular methods of wood carving. With just one knife, you can start making wood spoons, whistles and many other objects. Whittling is a very good starting points for wood carving beginners.


Chip carving normally needs two knifes, a primary and stab knife, like the wayne barton chip carving set, to make various geometric patterns. For relief carving, you carve figures in a flat panel of wood. The figures project slightly from the background rather than standing freely.


Of course, you can start whittling with just a pocket knife, but if you want to develop whittling into a hobby, it is a good idea to purchase a knife specially built for whittling. Since a good beginner starting knife is only $20, it is going to be a worthy investment.


A whittling knife is much more durable, much easier and comfortable to use, a whittling knife generally have a fixed blade and a longer handle. Also because carving through wood is certainly going to dull the blade, sharpening is stone is a necessary accessory for the tools of trade for whittling. What Wood Should I use?

You can use any wood that is available to you, that makes whittling a hobby very easy to maintain. There are a huge abundance of wood to choose from, and sometimes that may seems overwhelming to a beginner.


Woods for whittling are typically chosen for their softness and beauty, and also for their relative affordability. When choosing wood for whittling, you should avoid growth rings and knots. If you buy wood from a craft shop, this should be less of a problem, but if you are going to a lumber yard, please keep this in mind as it could be very difficult to cut around the growth rings.


Basswood has been used in woodworking for centuries, it is a white wood that grows throughout Europe and the Americas, it's from the Tilia genus.


Basswood is probably the most popular choice wood for beginners, it is a great wood to start to working on, since it is malleable, and relatively cheap. Basswood has almost no grain and is very soft, making it ideal for new woodworkers, it is often used for small model parts.


Balsa is another type of soft wood with a fine grain, Balsa is often used in the creation of model airplanes, like Basswood can be eaily found at your local craft hobby shop.
